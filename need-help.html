How to Reset Your Password

If you've forgotten your password, follow these simple steps to regain access to your account:

1. Request a Password Reset Link

Enter your registered email address below, and we’ll send you a password reset link. Click the link in the email to reset your password securely.

2. Create a New Password

Once you receive the reset link, click on it, and you will be redirected to a page where you can create a new password. For your security, your new password must meet the following criteria:

  • At least 8 characters long.
  • Includes both uppercase and lowercase letters.
  • Contains at least one number.
  • Has at least one special character (e.g., !, @, #, $, %).

3. Secure Your Account

After resetting your password, it's important to follow additional steps to secure your account:

  • Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): This will add an extra layer of security by requiring a second form of verification (such as a code sent to your phone or email).
  • Review Recent Login Activity: Check your account’s login history to ensure that no unauthorized users have accessed your account.
  • Update Security Questions: If applicable, update your account's security questions to ensure they’re unique and difficult for others to guess.

Tips for Keeping Your Password Secure

  • Use a Password Manager: A password manager can securely store your passwords and generate strong, unique passwords for each of your accounts.
  • Don't Share Your Password: Never share your password with anyone, not even friends or family.
  • Avoid Using Public Wi-Fi: Public Wi-Fi networks are more vulnerable to hackers. Avoid logging in to sensitive accounts when connected to public Wi-Fi.
  • Change Your Password Regularly: Set a reminder to change your passwords periodically, ideally every 3-6 months.
  • Be Wary of Phishing Scams: Always verify the source of any email or message before clicking on any links or downloading attachments.
  • Don’t Use the Same Password Everywhere: If one of your accounts is compromised, it puts all your other accounts at risk if you use the same password.

What to Do If You Suspect Your Account Has Been Hacked

If you believe your account has been compromised, follow these steps:

  • Change your password immediately to something strong and unique.
  • Log out of all sessions and re-login using your new password.
  • Check your account activity for any unauthorized actions or changes.
  • If your email is also compromised, change the password for your email account as well.
  • Contact customer support to report the issue and get additional help.

How to Recover Your Account If You Can’t Access Your Email

If you no longer have access to your email address, follow these steps to recover your account:

  • Check if you have any backup email address linked to your account.
  • If you have access to the phone number associated with the account, use it to recover your account.
  • Submit a request to customer support with details about your account, and we will assist you in regaining access.

Understanding Password Security Risks

When it comes to securing your account, understanding potential risks is crucial:

  • Data Breaches: If a website you use experiences a data breach, your password may be exposed. It’s important to change your password regularly and keep track of security incidents.
  • Brute-Force Attacks: Attackers use automated systems to guess passwords by trying various combinations. Strong passwords help protect you from such attacks.
  • Social Engineering: Hackers may attempt to trick you into revealing your password through fake emails or phone calls. Always verify the identity of anyone requesting sensitive information.
